Apoorva Srinivasan

"We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit." - Aristotle
Hi! My name is Apoorva Srinivasan. I’m a second-year Master’s student in Biostatistics at Columbia University’s Mailman School of Public Health. Prior to Columbia, I earned an engineering degree in biotechnology from Manipal Institute of Technology, India. Along the way, I've worked at a healthcare startup in NYC as the first recruit and various research institutions in India, Poland and USA.

Very early on, I was sucked into the mystical, fascinating world of literature and those reading habits have shaped most of what I am now. As a undergraduate student, I spent most of my time with people that loved words which lead me to cofound the first national debating tournament and The Writing Wing. During this time, I also spent a lot of time diving deep and devouring textbooks of my other love-- mathematics. Currently, I am deeply interested in computational psychiatry, thinking about the future of healthcare and learning and teaching yoga.
